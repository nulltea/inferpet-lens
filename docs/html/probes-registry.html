§01

Introduction

one name per measure

A probe is an attack-independent measure that correlates with attack recovery; it is never the attack reporting its own bits. Each probe reads one channel of the released representation and reports leakage in bits. This page fixes the name and symbol for each so the cross-surface comparison stays legible.

The site previously named the same measure several ways, and two distinct spectral quantities were both called spectral capacity. Two corrections are load-bearing. First, the geometry-only Gaussian ceiling I_G is a converse on embedding-inversion leakage; it is not an umbrella term for spectral probes. Second, the shared spectral capacity used as a secondary key/value diagnostic is a different quantity from I_G, computed on a different channel, and the two are not interchangeable. The table below is the single source for both.

All measures report on one comparable scale, bits, beside a per-secret human readout (perplexity, token-F1, recovery-rate, cosine, or membership AUC). The reporting convention lives in src/talens/report.py; the estimators live in src/talens/measures/.

§03

Two spectral quantities, not one

I_G vs shared spectral capacity

I_G and the shared spectral capacity share the waterfilling form ½ Σᵢ log₂(1 + λᵢ/σ²) but are different measures on different channels and must not be conflated.

Gaussian channel-capacity MI ceiling, I_G. Channel Y = e₀ + N(0, σ²I) on the released embedding; Σ = Cov(e₀) over the secret prior, σ² the defense noise. It is a converse: an upper bound on the embedding-inversion leakage that ceilings recovery for every attack. Matched probe for embedding inversion under the Gaussian mechanism.

Shared spectral capacity. Channel is the averaged row covariance C̄ = (1/T) Σₜ UₜUₜᵀ/d over a stack of T key/value observations; σ² is the median of the lower-half eigenvalues, an empirical floor. It is a secondary diagnostic for the joint-diagonalization accumulation attack, not a converse on text leakage. The key/value pages carry it beside the whitened-row negentropy J, never as I_G.

This is the I_G waterfilling picture: leakage is confined to the top eigendirections (T4), and the dashed σ² floor drowns the long tail (modes 21…2304, not plotted). Worked example on the gemma-2-2b codebook scatter S; the principle is the matched I_G probe’s per-mode decomposition. It is not the GTR embedding Cov(e₀) spectrum and does not resolve vec2text’s deferred empirical-localization claim.
