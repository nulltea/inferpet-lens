Bhattacharyya–Fano
— error bounds

A two-sided bound on the decode error of the embedding-DP channel: a union-Bhattacharyya upper bound and a Fano lower bound on the nearest-neighbour decode error, both from the codebook geometry and the noise alone. They bracket the attack's error from above and below.

probe reference · 2026-06-24
§01

Algorithm

inputs → transforms → two-sided error

The released embedding of a token is one of K codewords passed through a Gaussian channel. The bounds come from how close the codewords are and how large the noise is.

  1. Take the codebook geometry: the released embedding codewords {e₁, …, e_K} and the release noise σ.
  2. For each codeword pair compute the Bhattacharyya coefficient BC_{ij} = exp(−‖eᵢ − eⱼ‖² / 8σ²): the overlap of their noised distributions.
  3. Upper bound (union-Bhattacharyya): the MAP (nearest-neighbour) decode error is at most the averaged pairwise overlap, P_e^ub = (1/K) Σᵢ Σ_{j≠i} BC_{ij} (clipped to 1).
  4. Lower bound (Fano): from the channel equivocation, P_e^lb solves the Fano inequality given the mutual information the channel passes (no decoder can do better).

The output is the pair (P_e^lb, P_e^ub) bracketing the decode error, with the leakage in bits available from the matched mutual-information ceiling. The estimator is src/talens/measures/channel_error_bounds.py; the bracketing, the Fano equivocation, and the geometry-only independence are checked in tests/test_channel_error_bounds.py.

§02

Method

what it estimates · which surface · what it bounds

Kind. A two-sided bound on the decode error: an achievability-style upper bound (a specific decoder, nearest neighbour, cannot do worse than the union-Bhattacharyya sum) and a converse lower bound (no decoder can do better than Fano).

Surface. The embedding table released under the Gaussian mechanism, decoded by nearest-neighbour MAP. The matched attack is the Bayes nearest-neighbour decoder. It is defined per noise level σ.

What it bounds. It bounds the decode error P_e from both sides: P_e^lb ≤ P_e ≤ P_e^ub for the MAP decoder. The two bounds bracket the measured Bayes nearest-neighbour error across the noise sweep.

§03

Rationale

why attack-independent · where it tracks · the bracket

Attack-independent. Both bounds come from the codebook geometry (pairwise codeword distances) and the noise σ, never from the adversary's observations or its decoded output. They were adopted in place of a nearest-neighbour-style predictive probe precisely because that alternative was the attack in disguise; these bounds are geometry-only.

Where it tracks recovery. Across the dense Gaussian-DP sweep on the embedding table the two bounds bracket the Bayes nearest-neighbour error at all 10 of 10 noise points (P_e^lb ≤ P_e ≤ P_e^ub at every ε). The Fano lower bound rank-tracks the measured error at ρ = +0.937 and the union-Bhattacharyya upper bound at ρ = +0.888. See the Bayes-NN page.

The bracket is the value. A single converse (such as the I_G Fano ceiling) can be slack; pairing it with an achievability upper bound brackets the realized error, so the gap between the two is itself the diagnostic of how much room an attack has. The Fano arm is the converse; the union-Bhattacharyya arm is the matched achievability for the nearest-neighbour decoder.

§04

Plaintext reference

clean codebook · noise sweep from the low-noise (plaintext) end

On a clean codebook with no noise the decode is exact and both bounds are zero. The baseline is read from the low-noise end of the sweep, where the bounds depart from zero as σ grows.
