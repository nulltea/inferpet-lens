Cross-surface synthesis
— measured probe, recovery, utility

The measured sweep values for every surface on one page: probe bits, attack recovery, and utility where recorded, point by point across each defense or attack parameter, so the probe-versus-recovery relationship is visible and comparable across surfaces. The overview indexes the thirteen loops; the data section carries the numbers.

§01

Introduction

what this page aggregates

Each surface page runs one loop: measure leakage with an attack-independent probe in bits, run a recovery attack, and sweep a defense or attack parameter to test whether the bits predict the recovery. This page aggregates the measured sweeps. The overview (§02) gives one row per surface for the broad comparison; the data section (§03) gives the per-surface tables of measured probe bits, attack recovery, and utility at each sweep point, with the derived rank correlation beneath each.

The comparable quantity across surfaces is the rank correlation between probe bits and attack recovery over the sweep, written ρ (Spearman), with a success bar at |ρ| ≥ 0.6 fixed across all surfaces. Absolute recovery is not cross-comparable: secret kinds, readouts, and base models differ (gemma-2-2b for capacity, differential privacy, permutation, and the implemented defenses; Qwen3-4B for the residual and key-value defenses; GTR-T5-base for the sentence-embedding surfaces). Each table reduces a multi-axis sweep to a representative one-dimensional slice along the load-bearing parameter, holding the other axes fixed at the value named in the caption; where a depth axis matters, representative layers are shown separately. Every number is quoted from the surface's recorded results file, cited in the caption.

Verdict vocabulary: tracks (ρ ≥ 0.6, or bounds that bracket the error); attack-limited (a weak attack decorrelates, a stronger one re-correlates); probe not matched (the probe reads a channel other than the load-bearing leak); vacuous (capacity exceeds the secret entropy, so the probe is constant over the operating range).

The probe names above are distinct measures, not synonyms or subtypes of one another; the matched probe differs by surface. Spectral channel-MI is the closed-form Gaussian-channel ceiling I_G = ½ Σᵢ log₂(1 + λᵢ/σ²) computed from the embedding covariance spectrum and the noise variance, geometry-only with no attack. CLUB is a learned variational upper bound on the same mutual information. Capacity-matched PVI is the top-1 accuracy of a capacity-bounded token-identity reader. Whitened-row negentropy is the source-separation separability resource. The Bayes-NN bounds are a union-Bhattacharyya upper bound and a Fano lower bound on the decode error. The spectral capacity column in the KV-Cloak table is a separate shared-spectral-capacity diagnostic, not spectral channel-MI.

§04

Cross-cutting findings

claims spanning surfaces; proofs pending

1: a channel-matched, attack-independent probe predicts recovery on the majority of surfaces (Supported). ρ ≥ 0.6 on nine of thirteen surfaces, spanning token-identity reading (depth inversion +0.85, split inference +0.958), embedding inversion (Vec2Text +1.00, Bayes-NN bracketed), source separation against a matched floor (+0.92, +0.77), and permutation matching (+0.976). Single seed and single model per surface; the correlation holds within a sweep and a defense family, but the absolute calibration does not transfer across families or models. Proof: pending (claim:cross-surface-matched-probe-tracks-recovery).

2: every failure resolves to a probe not matched to the load-bearing channel or to a vacuous capacity probe (Supported). Stained Glass across shapes (+0.48, while read-subspace distortion is +0.97) is probe-not-matched, proven structurally (the scalar capacity is allocation-blind at fixed budget); GELO (+0.29–0.51) is a weak loop with the same likely diagnosis but the stronger-attack arm not yet ruled out; Rep2Text (+0.18) is vacuous (capacity ≫ entropy, extraction-limited). Each carries a queued follow-up; the GELO and Rep2Text diagnoses are classified, not yet demonstrated by re-correlation. Proof: pending (claim:probe-failure-dichotomy-matched-or-vacuous).

3: attack strength, not the measure, governs whether present information is realized (Supported). On the same surface a weak linear attack decorrelates while a stronger one re-correlates: input-DP at depth (ridge −0.09 vs learned +0.83), the input layer (Bayes-NN +0.98 uplift), the permutation table (full sorted-row matcher +0.60, ρ +0.976). Single seed; the attack class is not exhausted, so the evidence is asymmetric. Proof: pending (claim:attack-strength-governs-realized-leakage).

4: deployed defenses concentrate privacy in a single load-bearing parameter a matched probe localizes (Supported). KV-Cloak feature mix (token mix and block size inert), PriPert perturbation β (collapses by β ≈ 0.5), AloePri dense key cover (core breakable to α_e ≈ 0.35). No scalar privacy level reduces leakage uniformly across secret kinds (Shredder: permutation 1.00 → 0.04 while token identity holds above 0.45). Proof: pending (claim:defense-privacy-is-single-channel-localizable).

The thesis holds wherever the probe is matched to the load-bearing channel and the attack is strong enough to realize the present information. The two failure modes are instances of those preconditions being unmet, each bounded by theory on its surface page. The cryptographic zero-leakage schemes on the index anchor the floor below this entire comparison.
