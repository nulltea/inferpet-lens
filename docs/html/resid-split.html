Split inference under PriPert
— a matched measure tracks the attack, but its certificate is slack

A split-inference adversary observes the residual activation at a cut layer of a 4-billion-parameter model. The PriPert defense sparsifies that activation and adds a bounded perturbation. Across a sweep of split layer, sparsity ratio, and perturbation budget, a matched information measure computed without any attack predicts token-inversion recovery at a rank correlation of 0.96, and tracks a stronger learned inverter at 0.92. The same measure gives a valid error converse with no violations across 32 settings, yet that converse is numerically slack: it binds only where recovery has already fallen to near zero.

research report · 2026-06-24
§01

Introduction

the defense, the threat model, the headline

Split inference cuts a network at one layer and sends the intermediate activation to an untrusted server. PriPert (arXiv 2605.23158) defends that cut with two operations: activation sparsification, which keeps only the largest-magnitude coordinates of each token position, and an additive perturbation bounded by a per-row energy budget. The paper proves a reconstruction-error converse. This report applies the defense to the residual stream of Qwen3-4B and asks the question this project asks of every surface: does an attack-independent information measure predict how far the inversion attack succeeds as the defense parameters vary, and does its formal converse bind in the regime where the defense is actually used?

The perturbation is realized as the channel-matched isotropic Gaussian whose capacity the matched measure is the converse of, with energy fixed to the plaintext activation scale. This is the attack-independent realization: it uses no inverter, so the defense is never defined by the attack the library then runs against it. The fully adversarial perturbation that PriPert optimizes against a surrogate inverter is the faithful upper-strength variant and is recorded as out of scope for this per-position pipeline.

§03

Method

capture, defense Transform, sweep, inverters, measures

Residual activations for 512 prompts were reused from a cached capture at four split layers. PriPert was implemented as a representation transform applied to each captured activation before the attacks and measures observe it: per row, the smallest-magnitude coordinates are zeroed to leave a fraction ρ, then independent Gaussian noise of standard deviation β times the mean plaintext row scale is added. The noise scale is referenced to the plaintext activation and held fixed as ρ varies, so that sparsification reduces signal against a constant noise floor.

Two sweeps were run. A perturbation sweep varies β over 0, 0.25, 0.5, 1, and 2 at fixed ρ = 0.25, across all four layers. A sparsity sweep varies ρ over 1, 0.25, and 0.05 at fixed β = 0.25, across all four layers. A plaintext setting at ρ = 1, β = 0 anchors each layer. Each of the 32 resulting settings is attacked by all three inverters under the vocabulary-disjoint split with a label-shuffle control and a bootstrap interval, and measured by the matched spectral channel mutual information and the contrastive bound. The measures read only the sparsified covariance and the noise level, never the inverter output, so they are attack-independent by construction.

§04

The matched measure and its converse

channel mutual information, accessible bits, Fano ceiling

For the Gaussian channel that adds noise of variance σ² to the sparsified activation with covariance eigenvalues λi, the channel mutual information is the sum over modes of one half the base-two logarithm of one plus λi over σ². This is a maximum-entropy upper bound on the information any observer can hold about the activation, and by the data-processing inequality an upper bound on information about the token. Combined with the token entropy of about 11 bits over the candidate pool, it gives an accessible-bit count and, through Fano's inequality, a ceiling on the top-1 recovery of any estimator. The token entropy uses an idealized uniform prior over the candidate pool; the evaluated secret is the test-row token, whose true distribution is not uniform, so the empirical ceiling is at least as slack as reported. The measure is therefore both a predictor of recovery and a formal converse, computed from geometry and the noise budget alone.

§05

Results

suppression, measure tracking, the converse

The perturbation is the dominant parameter and its effect depends on depth. At a fixed sparsity of ρ = 0.25, recovery falls steeply with the perturbation budget at the middle layers and more slowly at the deepest sampled layer, while the first layer resists. The first layer is the post-block-0 residual, input-adjacent and with a small plaintext scale, so the fixed-scale noise removes little: even at β = 2 its recovery stays at 0.363. The sparsity ratio is the secondary parameter and moves recovery appreciably only at its smallest value.

The matched measure tracks recovery across the joint sweep at a rank correlation of 0.958, and tracks the stronger learned inverter at 0.915, so no measure-attack gap is observed for the tested inverters. The within-layer perturbation sweep at the three responsive layers is perfectly rank-ordered, which the theory below explains. The eight noiseless settings (β = 0) have an infinite measure and are excluded from the rank correlations.

The Fano ceiling has no violations across all 32 settings: measured recovery never exceeds the converse. Yet the ceiling equals one, and so constrains nothing, in 31 of 32 settings, because the measure stays far above the 11-bit idealized token entropy across the useful range. It falls below that entropy in exactly one setting, layer 8 at β = 2, where the measure is 10.0 bits and recovery is already 0.002. Under the true non-uniform test-token distribution the ceiling is looser still, so the slack conclusion only strengthens.

§06

Findings

claims, grounds, scope

PriPert suppresses inversion, perturbation-first and depth-dependent (Supported). The additive perturbation is the dominant parameter; sparsification is secondary. Recovery collapses to near zero by β = 0.5 at layers 8 and 16 and by β = 1 at layer 24, while the input-adjacent first layer resists even the largest budget. The effect is therefore not a uniform property of the defense but depends on where the cut is taken.

The matched measure predicts recovery, including the stronger attack (Supported). Computed with no inverter, the spectral channel mutual information tracks recovery across the joint sweep at 0.958, holds at 0.916 once the perturbation axis is held fixed, and reaches the stronger learned inverter at 0.915. The measure is a monotone predictor of recovery here, not a tight one.

The converse is valid but slack (Supported, with the slack as the finding). The Fano ceiling has no violations, so it is a sound converse, but it is numerically vacuous across almost the entire sweep and binds only after recovery has already fallen to near zero. The empirical recovery floor is reached far inside the information-theoretic converse.

§07

Theory

validity, perturbation-axis co-monotonicity, slack

Three results, proof-checked across models, separate what the measure proves from what it only predicts. First, the measure is a valid converse: along the chain from token to sparsified activation to observation, the data-processing inequality and the Gaussian maximum-entropy bound cap the token information at the measure, and Fano's inequality turns that into a ceiling on the recovery of any estimator. This certifies the absence of violations.

Second, along the perturbation axis at fixed sparsity, both the measure and the Bayes-optimal recovery are non-increasing in the noise level: more noise lowers the measure by direct differentiation, and lowers optimal recovery because added independent noise is a valid degradation of the observation. Two quantities that are both monotone in the same parameter share a ranking, which is the structural reason the within-layer perturbation sweep is perfectly rank-ordered. This argument holds only along the perturbation axis. Monotonicity in the sparsity ratio is false in general for the magnitude mask, because which coordinates survive is itself informative; the joint and sparsity-axis correlations are therefore empirical, not theorems.

Third, the converse is slack by a counting argument. The ceiling constrains recovery only when the measure falls below the token entropy, which forces the number of surviving spectral modes below about twice that entropy, roughly twenty modes. A recovery attack reads the token from a few predictive directions and fails once those are overwhelmed by noise, long before all but about twenty of the thousands of modes fall below the noise level. On this high-rank residual the noise that floors recovery leaves the measure far above the token entropy, so the converse cannot bind in the regime where the defense is used.

§08

Discussion

predictor versus certificate, scope, follow-up

On this surface the measure is useful as a predictor of the attack, not as a certificate. It rank-tracks recovery across the joint defense sweep and follows even the stronger learned inverter, so an analyst can infer the defense setting from the measure without running an attack. Its formal converse is sound but loose, binding only after the perturbation has already removed recovery. This repeats, for a perturbation defense, the slack-converse pattern found earlier on the single-layer residual, where capacity was likewise non-binding while the measure still predicted recovery.

Scope. The findings are for one model, one corpus, and a single noise seed, with the perturbation realized as the channel-matched Gaussian rather than the adversarial perturbation PriPert optimizes, and with intervals over test rows rather than seeds. The sparsity-axis and joint correlations are empirical; only the perturbation-axis tracking is proved. The first-layer resistance, multiple seeds, a second model, and the adversarial perturbation are the remaining follow-ups.
