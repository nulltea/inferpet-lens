Shared spectral capacity
— key/value diagnostic

A secondary key/value diagnostic for the joint-diagonalization accumulation channel: the spectral capacity of the averaged row covariance across an observation stack against an empirical noise floor. It is not the Gaussian channel-capacity MI ceiling, a different channel and a different quantity.

probe reference · 2026-06-24
§01

Algorithm

inputs → transforms → bits

The accumulation attack averages many key/value observations to expose shared structure. This diagnostic measures the capacity of that averaged covariance against the floor the averaging leaves behind.

  1. Take a stack of T key/value observations and form the averaged row covariance C̄ = (1/T) Σₜ UₜUₜᵀ / d.
  2. Eigendecompose C̄ to its eigenvalues λ₁ ≥ … ≥ λ_d.
  3. Set the noise floor σ² to the median of the lower-half eigenvalues, an empirical floor for the averaged stack.
  4. Sum the per-mode capacity above that floor, ½ Σᵢ log₂(1 + λᵢ/σ²), to the shared spectral capacity in bits.

The form is the same water-filling sum as the I_G ceiling, but the channel and the floor are different (see §02). The estimator lives in src/talens/measures/bss_separability.py beside the whitened-row negentropy probe; it is reported only as a key/value secondary diagnostic.

§02

Method

what it estimates · which surface · not I_G

Kind. A secondary spectral diagnostic for the key/value accumulation channel, not a converse on text leakage. It indexes how much shared structure survives averaging over the observation stack.

Surface. The joint-diagonalization accumulation channel on key/value rows. It accompanies the whitened-row negentropy probe on the key/value pages and is reported beside it, never in its place.

Not the Gaussian MI ceiling. It shares the water-filling form ½ Σᵢ log₂(1 + λᵢ/σ²) with I_G, but the channel and floor differ: here the covariance is the averaged row covariance C̄ over a stack of T observations, and σ² is the empirical median of the lower-half eigenvalues, not a defense noise variance. I_G is a converse on embedding-inversion text leakage; this is an accumulation-channel diagnostic. The two are different measures and must not be conflated, as the registry sets out.

§03

Rationale

why attack-independent · its secondary role

Attack-independent. The capacity is computed from the averaged covariance spectrum and an empirical floor; it never runs the demix and never reads the recovered sources.

Secondary role. The matched primary probe for the source-separation channel is the whitened-row negentropy J, which tracks the genuine separation margin. The shared spectral capacity reports a complementary view of how much shared spectral structure the accumulation has to work with: useful for ranking accumulation channels, not as a converse on text recovery. Under KV-Cloak it moves with the channel transform (about 18 bits on the identity channel, about 21 bits with the feature mix only, about 16 bits with the full defense), beside the negentropy probe's collapse on the feature-mix channels.
