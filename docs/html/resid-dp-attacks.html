Residual stream
— under differential privacy

When a defense adds noise to a token's representation, does the leakage the MI probes see actually get recovered by an attack? The gap between the two is an attack-efficiency gap, not a probe failure. It appears in input-DP settings — and at depth specifically requires noise propagation (it is absent under at-layer noise, where even ridge tracks MI). A weak ridge attack collapses and decorrelates from MI; an information-efficient attack realizes the preserved information (exact Bayes-NN at L0, the ceiling-realization case) and re-correlates with MI (a trained channel-aware decoder at propagated depth).

§01

Introduction

the thesis on one surface

The leakage probes (mutual-information estimators) barely move as differential-privacy noise grows, yet a deployed ridge attack's recovery collapses. Is the probe wrong, or is the attack weak? The attack is weak. The information the probe measures is preserved; the weak attack just cannot extract it — and a stronger, information-efficient attack does, re-correlating recovery with the probe.

Threat model — WEIGHTS-PUB honest-but-curious. The adversary knows the model weights, the embedding table, and the published DP parameters (clip C, noise σ), and can synthesize unlimited (noised-representation, token) training pairs at any σ. Every attack compared here uses only this — comparisons between admissible attacks are fair (claim:threat-model-fairness).

The one finding that frames the page: the MI↔recovery decorrelation (ridge recovery anti-correlating with the MI probes across the sweep) is not universal — at depth it is specific to noise propagation. Under noise added at the captured layer, even the weak ridge tracks MI perfectly (ρ=1.0). At L0 ridge still tracks the probes positively (+0.76..+0.88) though its recovery collapses (an uplift gap, not decorrelation). True decorrelation (ridge ρ=−0.09) appears only when DP noise is injected at the embedding and reshaped through the nonlinear blocks; there a stronger decoder is needed, and it re-correlates (+0.83).

§04

The probes

why they are attack-independent

All three probes are computed without running the attack (integrity-critical — a probe that reported the attack's own bits would make any correlation circular). The cross-model integrity audit confirmed CLUB, V_cap and SDL each estimate leakage independently and do not call the attack's recovery output.

Under at-layer noise all three track recovery (Spearman 0.80–1.0): CLUB and V_cap are smoothest (ρ=1.0), SDL noisier and 6–7× costlier (report it auxiliary). This closes the "is MDL faithful too?" question and shows the probes are not the limiting factor — the attack is.

R7 — does the V_cap probe track recovery across depth? · the depth-decoupling finding (single seed, relocated from the retired capacity-pvi page; source: refine-logs/resid-capacity-pvi/RESULTS_STANDARDIZED.md)

§06

Claims

claims and their support

claim:restore-correlation: info-efficient attacks restore the MI↔recovery correlation where the weak attack decorrelates. Status: Supported at the input layer, Preliminary at propagated depth (single setting). The input layer supports the attack-efficiency premise via uplift and ceiling-realization (Bayes-NN +0.98); propagated depth supplies the literal re-correlation evidence (deep decoder +0.83 against ridge −0.09); the effect is explicitly absent under at-layer noise (no gap); a full-noise-range depth ceiling attack is open. research-wiki/claims/restore-correlation.md

claim:bayes-gap-diagnosis: the MI-recovery gap is a Bayes-optimality (attack-efficiency) gap, not poor MI prediction. Status: Supported at the input layer, Preliminary at depth. Shown directly at the input layer (the probe is high while ridge collapses and Bayes recovers near 1.0); at depth the gap is inferred, not measured to the ceiling. research-wiki/claims/bayes-gap-diagnosis.md

claim:depth-decoupling-input-dp: under propagated input-DP the attack-independent V_cap probe's tracking of the embedding-reconstruction attack is depth-resolved — it attenuates monotonically with depth (ρ +0.96 L0 → −0.21 L20, sign-reversing at L20) while CLUB attenuates in parallel but stays positive (+0.29 at L20). Status: scoped / jury PARTIAL, single seed. The parallel CLUB attenuation makes the depth-attenuation a signal property; the L20 sign reversal is readout-specific. This is the canonical decoupling example the matched-probe program cites (R7 above). research-wiki/claims/depth-decoupling-input-dp.md

The first two rest on the verified theorem claim:thm-t1-info-efficient (Bayes-optimal weakly dominates lossy/linear; recovery monotone in MI on the Gaussian arm): they are its empirical instantiation on the residual stream.

§07

Analysis

the verified theorem under the empirics

Theorem 1 (verified): under the S–X–Y Markov chain with finite second moment / finite alphabet, (a) the Bayes-optimal attack on Y weakly dominates any lossy/linear attack for every prior and stated loss (Blackwell post-processing / Rao–Blackwell); (b) strict for squared loss unless E[S|Y] is affine (MMSE orthogonality); (c) on the Gaussian/DP arm the optimal attack's recovery is monotone in MI (I-MMSE: dI/dsnr = ½·mmse; degradation-order DPI for the token target).

This is why the empirical result reads as attack efficiency: the optimal attack must track MI by Theorem 1(c), while a weak attack carries an additional approximation-gap term and need not. The full proof is in research-wiki/claims/thm-t1-info-efficient.md.

§08

Conclusions

what leaks, how much, why

What leaks. Under DP the token-identity information measured by MI is largely preserved (CLUB decays slowly); whether it is recovered is an attack-strength question. The anti-correlation between bits and recovery seen at depth is a property of noise propagation, not of MI or of noise per se (under at-layer noise even ridge tracks MI; at L0 the gap is uplift, not anti-correlation).

The honest gap. No single attack dominates the noise range — forward-model match wins low-noise (+0.53), the trained decoder wins high-noise. The matched program's next move is a noise-aware FMV ⊕ denoiser with multi-seed dense-ε confidence intervals; this consolidation is single-seed at depth and makes no robustness claim there.

Dead-ends recorded (first-class): plain MLP-vs-ridge under at-layer noise (no gap); embedding-space Vec2Text iteration (null, fixed point at t=1); Vec2Text on per-position resid (category error — it needs a pooled bottleneck, moved to the pooled-embedding surface). See research-wiki/experiments/resid-dp-attacks-negative-results.md.
