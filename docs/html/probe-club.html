Variational MI bound
— probe CLUB

A learned variational upper bound on the mutual information between the released representation and the token embedding. It brackets leakage from above with a trained network, without solving the inversion the attack performs.

probe reference · 2026-06-24
§01

Algorithm

inputs → transforms → bits

CLUB (Contrastive Log-ratio Upper Bound, Cheng et al., ICML 2020) bounds I(R; E) between the released representation R and the token embedding E using a learned conditional q_θ(e | r).

  1. Collect paired samples (r, e): released representations and their token embeddings.
  2. Train an approximate conditional q_θ(e | r) (a small network) on the positive pairs by maximum likelihood.
  3. Evaluate the contrastive upper bound: the mean log-likelihood on matched pairs minus the mean log-likelihood on mismatched pairs, CLUB = E_{p(r,e)}[log q_θ(e|r)] − E_{p(r)p(e)}[log q_θ(e|r)].
  4. Report the bound in bits, with the shuffle-control floor (mismatched labels) subtracted to give selectivity. The estimator is capped (max_rows = 2500, fixed step budget) to keep cost bounded.

The output is in bits. Beside it the report renders the per-secret readout for the surface (token recovery rate). The estimator is src/talens/measures/club.py; its stability under the cap is checked in tests/test_club_stability.py.

§02

Method

what it estimates · which surface · what it bounds

Kind. A mutual-information upper bound (learned). Unlike the geometry-only I_G ceiling, CLUB trains a network and so carries estimator variance, but it targets the same quantity from any representation, not just Gaussian-channel embeddings.

Surface. Any released representation paired with a token target: the residual stream under input-layer differential privacy, at-layer noise, the depth sweep, and the permutation-cover embedding table. It is defined per layer and per privacy parameter.

What it bounds. It upper-bounds I(R; E), the leakage of the token through the released representation. By the sufficiency identity that holds on the embedding surface, that mutual information equals the text leakage. It is a single scalar with no per-mode structure, so it does not localize where leakage lives.

§03

Rationale

why attack-independent · where it tracks · where it is loose

Attack-independent. CLUB fits q_θ(e | r) to predict the token embedding, not to reconstruct the attack's target output. It never runs the inverter and its shuffle-control floor is near zero at every layer, so the bound it reports is not the attack recovery in disguise.

Where it tracks recovery. Across the 36-layer control sweep it is a near-perfect rank predictor of recovery (Spearman 0.987, r² 0.96 over 108 blocks). Under the Gaussian-DP embedding sweep it matches I_G at ρ = +1.00, on the permutation cover it tracks the strong matcher at ρ = +0.976, and on the depth sweep ρ = +0.78. Under propagated input-layer differential privacy it attenuates with depth but stays positive (+0.96 at layer 0 to +0.29 at layer 20), where the V_cap reader decouples to −0.21: identity stays decodable while reconstruction does not.

Where it is loose. CLUB is a single scalar and is non-localizing: it reports how much leaks, not which directions or channels carry it. It also costs more than the geometry-only ceiling (it trains a network, roughly 1.3 s per block against about 60 ms for I_G) and inherits the variance of a learned estimator.
