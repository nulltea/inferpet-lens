Capacity-matched V-info
— probe V_cap

The accuracy and bits of a capacity-bounded token-identity reader on a class-prior null. Capacity-matching repairs the estimator so its shuffle-control floor is well-posed, and the bounded reader accuracy tracks inversion recovery where the representation is the bottleneck.

probe reference · 2026-06-24
§01

Algorithm

inputs → transforms → bits

The token-identity reader. For a released residual-stream vector X = resid_post[L] ∈ ℝ^d whose secret is the token identity behind it:

  1. Standardize X on training statistics (mean and variance from the train split only).
  2. Reduce to dim < n_val by a train-only principal-component projection (covariance eigendecomposition: the capacity-matching step, and the single change from the original failing estimator).
  3. Fit a calibrated linear-softmax classifier over the token-identity classes.
  4. Read out two quantities: reader accuracy, the bounded top-1 recovery over the token-identity classes (the robust primary readout); and reader information in bits, the predictive V-information (the log-probability the reader assigns the true token minus the prior log-probability) reported as selectivity (real minus label-shuffled control).

It never reads the embedding table: it predicts a class label, not a vector. That is what makes it attack-independent with respect to the embedding-inversion attack. The estimator is src/talens/measures/vinfo_capacity.py; it is checked in tests/test_vinfo_capacity.py.

FIG. 01 — measurement loop: released residual, attack arm (→ recovery) and probe arm (standardize → PCA dim<n → softmax → accuracy + bits) navy = data owner · plum = defense locus · slate = adversary/analyst · amber = leak · joined by Spearman(accuracy, recovery)

The probe and the attack run as independent arms from the same released vector. Their outputs are compared only after the fact, by the rank correlation Spearman(reader accuracy, recovery); the probe never reads the attack output.

§02

Method

what it estimates · which surface · what it bounds

Kind. V-information (predictive, capacity-bounded), reported as bounded reader accuracy with auxiliary bits. The capacity bound (dimension below the validation count) is what makes the estimator well-posed when feature dimension exceeds the validation sample.

Surface. Residual-stream vectors resid_post[L], and at layer 0 the noised input embedding. It is layer-defined, so it has a per-layer reading.

What it bounds. The capacity-bounded reader accuracy is a realizable lower bound on token-identity decodability for a reader of that capacity; the bits selectivity estimates the predictive V-information of the token identity given the representation.

Estimator repair — capacity matching (layer 12, gemma-2-2b, single seed)

The original reader failed as a bits estimator: with more features than validation points it interpolated, and its information on a label-shuffled control fell to roughly −50 bits at every depth, an artifact rather than a leakage signal. Reducing the feature dimension below the validation count bounds that control near −1 bit at every depth, at about 0.57 times the cost. This table is an estimator-calibration diagnostic: the bits columns compare reader families' real-vs-shuffle information floors, so the bits value is itself the subject and there is no per-secret recovery target to pair it with. §03

Rationale

why attack-independent · where it tracks · where it decouples

Attack-independent. It never reads the embedding table: it predicts a class label, not a vector. Its correlation with a retrieval-style reader is 0.66 to 0.76, below the redundancy threshold. The one change from the failing version (dimensionality reduction) adds no attack information.

Accuracy-primary, bits-auxiliary. The bits have no lower bound over readers (a single confidently-wrong assignment sends the quantity to negative infinity), so they are fragile; bounded accuracy stays in [0,1] and is the object that tracks the attack. Report the bits as auxiliary.

Where it tracks recovery. Under at-layer representation-space defenses (principal-component-subspace ablation, isotropic hidden-state noise) the bounded reader accuracy tracks the ridge inversion attack at Spearman ρ 0.80 to 1.00 at every depth: the positive, attack-predictive regime. This tracking evidence, with the input-DP results, lives on the differential-privacy page.

Where it decouples (the canonical example). Under propagated input-layer differential privacy the tracking attenuates monotonically with depth (ρ +0.96 at layer 0 to −0.21 at layer 20), while CLUB attenuates in parallel but stays positive (+0.96 to +0.29). Identity stays decodable while reconstruction does not. This is the matched-probe program's canonical decoupling example; the claim node depth-decoupling-input-dp and the differential-privacy results own the measured tables.

Why the repair works — three propositions

Lemma 1. The probe's information in bits is the log-probability the reader assigns the true token minus the prior log-probability. Because a probability can approach zero, this quantity has no lower bound over readers: a single confidently-wrong assignment sends it to negative infinity. Accuracy, an indicator average, stays in the unit interval, so accuracy cannot blow up while the bits can.

Proposition 2. With more features than validation points the data is separable, and an unregularized log-loss fit interpolates (training probabilities approach 1). On a shuffled control the confident prediction disagrees with the permuted label on at least one point, the assigned probability there approaches zero, and the empirical control information (a finite average with one diverging term) falls without bound. This is the mechanism of the observed −50-bit floor, not a derivation of its exact value.

Proposition 3. If the reader's logits are bounded by a cap fixed independently of the feature dimension, every class probability is at least a positive softmax floor, so the information is bounded below by a finite, dimension-independent constant. Dimensionality reduction does not enter this bound directly; it is the practical device that keeps the fit out of the interpolation regime so a modest logit cap is attainable, which is why the repair tracks the dimension rather than the regularization strength.

Full proof: research-wiki/claims/capacity-matched-pvi.md (estimator lemma verified).

§05

Scope and limits

what the evidence does and does not cover

The estimator-repair evidence is a single model (gemma-2-2b), a single seed, seven privacy points per layer, with no confidence intervals. The Gaussian class-conditional reader stays miscalibrated even after dimensionality reduction: capacity matching is necessary but not sufficient, the reader must also be calibrated. The bits readout is calibration-sensitive and reported as auxiliary. Named next steps: multiple seeds with intervals, cross-model replication, sensitivity at a smaller reduced dimension, and a calibration diagnostic on the bits-fragile cells.
